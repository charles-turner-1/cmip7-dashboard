**abrupt-4xCO2** is an idealised stress-test. Starting from the pre-industrial
control climate, the carbon dioxide concentration is **instantly quadrupled** and
then held there while the model runs on.

## What it does

Nothing about this is meant to be realistic — no real scenario quadruples CO2
overnight. That is the point. By applying one large, clean, well-defined push, we
can watch the climate system respond without the clutter of volcanoes, aerosols
and gradually changing emissions getting in the way.

## Why we care

This experiment is how we measure some of the most important numbers in climate
science:

- **Climate sensitivity** — how much the planet ultimately warms for a given
  increase in CO2.
- **Feedbacks** — how clouds, water vapour, ice and the like amplify or damp
  that warming.
- How quickly the ocean takes up heat, which sets the *pace* of warming.

Because every model runs exactly the same idealised experiment, `abrupt-4xCO2`
gives us a clean way to compare one model against another.
