The **historical** experiment is the model's attempt to reproduce the climate we
have actually observed, from the mid-19th century up to the near-present. It is
one of the cornerstone simulations every model contributes to CMIP.

## What it does

Starting from a stable pre-industrial state, the model is driven forward using
our best reconstruction of the real-world *forcings* over that period:

- rising greenhouse gas concentrations,
- volcanic eruptions and changes in solar output,
- aerosols and other pollutants,
- land-use change.

Everything else — the winds, ocean currents, the timing of El Niño — the model
works out for itself.

## Why we care

The historical run is how we check the model against reality. If it can
reproduce the observed warming, the pattern of temperature change, and events
like the cooling after major volcanic eruptions, we gain confidence that its
projections of the *future* are worth taking seriously. It is also the baseline
that future scenario runs branch off from.
